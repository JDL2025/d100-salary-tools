The Core Problem: Inflation Outpaced Every Raise

Prices rose 44.7% from 2010 to 2026. D100 salaries in the Bachelor's lane rose only 38.1% at Step 1 ‚Äî and only 37.7% in the Master's lane at Step 10. Teachers received raises every year, but those raises never kept up with the cost of living. The gap isn't just a number ‚Äî it represents groceries, rent, gas, and healthcare that teachers can no longer afford the way they once could.

The Bargaining Bottom Line

When the district says teachers are earning "more than ever," they are technically correct in nominal terms. But nominal dollars don't buy groceries, pay rent, or fill gas tanks. Every D100 teacher ‚Äî at every step, in both the Bachelor's and Master's lanes ‚Äî is earning less in real purchasing power than their 2010 counterpart. The district has not simply failed to give adequate raises; it has quietly reduced real compensation year after year while handing teachers a number that looks bigger on paper.
